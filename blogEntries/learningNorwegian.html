How I'm Learning Norwegian

Why I am learning Norwegian

I have always wanted to learn a secound language ever since high school. However, I have had several false starts on trying to learn a foreign language (… High school Spanish). After learning more about my family history, I wanted to know more about them since I was not told anything when I was younger. The main thing I wanted to learn through is the languages they spoke so I could read all the documents that were in all those boxes in the attic. So, this year I have made a good amount of progress by really focusing on a proper study session with Norwegian. By the end of the year I hope to have reached A2 status. As time goes by, I will post my progress on here to show you what has changed.

My Daily Rountine

Currently I use one textbook for learning Norwegian called “The mystery of Nils”. This in my opinion is the best book I have seen to learn Norwegian because of its use of an engaging story, and realistic method of teaching grammar. Alongside with that I use a premade flashcard list made on memerise to practice the vocabulary. I find learning ten words a day is a manageable amount of words that slowly builds a strong vocabulary base. So, my routine starts with doing a set or two of vocab review on memrise; I move on to learning ten words after that. Once I am done with my vocabulary I either do some exercises in the book or translate a passage. If I have any extra time, I will find another source to practice listening or translation a passage.
