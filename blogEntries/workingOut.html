Working Out

Backstory

Before this year (2020) I have not worked out besides the occasional class in school or social activity. That is why this year I decided to do something different to improve my life. I ran into a YouTube channel "Minus the Gym" while looking into the One Punch man workout. Minus the Gym focuses on workout routines that require little to no equipment. Which if you do not know is falls into calisthenics. I have been doing calisthenics for a few months now, and it has made quite the difference. I am not built or anything, but I do feel an increase in my strength, and I feel better about myself. I really recommend getting into calisthenics due to it being accessible to just about anyone.

My Rountine

Currently I am doing four sets of the routine listed below. This calisthenics routine is enough that a beginner will start to see some improvement within a month if you are doing it every other day. Although I am at lower levels of most of the exercises in this routine, I like how easy it is to scale the difficulty of each exercise to fit your needs. Most of the time I like to increase the difficulty of each exercise rather than add a set. If I know I can complete every set with the harder level I will only do the first set at the harder level. If you want to start out, I suggest starting with one set on the easiest level of all four exercises and then making it more difficult every two weeks or so.

General Insructions

  • Start whenever you need to in the progression for each section. For example, some people might start with 1 for pull, push, and hinge, and 2 for squat.
  • Do reps until failure or you hit the target. The target is listed with the movement (eg pull-ups x10) Push and Hinge targets are all 10 reps.
  • Between reps, take a few deep breaths and start the next movement immediately if able. If you need more time between reps, then take it. Remember, quality over quantity.
  • Rest for at least two minutes between circuits when first starting out. As you progress, reduce the rest to 1-2 minutes.
  • Level up when you are able to hit the target number of reps for a movement for all four circuits

Side Note

For each exercise I have included a video tutorial that should have the proper form of doing each exercise. I am not a personal trainer or a health expert so if you feel something is wrong make sure to talk to a trusted source. If you happen to see a exercise video that has the wrong form let me know via my contact page, and I will update it.

Warm up

To be safe you should always do some streaching, and then pick one of the following:

  1. Jumpting Jacks
  2. Burpees
  3. Mountain Climbers
  4. Jump rope

Pull

Do ten reps of whichever level you are currentely on:

  1. Bent-leg bodyweight rows with dip bar
  2. Straight-leg bodyweight rows with dip bar
  3. Pull up negatives x5
  4. Assisted pull-ups (with resistance band)
  5. Pull-ups
  6. Tucked front-level pull-ups

Squat

  1. Half squat (thighs almost parallel) x30
  2. Full squat x30 (optional calf raises)
  3. Hop squat x20
  4. Lunge x20
  5. Switching lunges
  6. Tuck-jump squats

Push

Do ten reps of whichever level you are currentely on:

  1. Wall push-ups
  2. Incline push-ups (use stairs, chair, etc
  3. Standard push-ups
  4. Diamond push-ups
  5. Archer push-ups
  6. Clapping push-ups

Hinge

Do ten reps of whichever level you are currently on:

  1. Lying Tucks (lying on the floor with legs extended, tuck your knees into your chest)
  2. Lying leg raises
  3. Hanging Knee raises
  4. Hanging leg raises
  5. Inverted leg raises (headstand with leg 'raises')

Cool down

Do some light movement, but don't just sit on the couch as that can be bad for your health.
